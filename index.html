Development of Speech and Language

Speech & language is an essential part of any child's development. Language development impacts your child's social interactions, behavior, and academic skills.

Speech Milestones (Birth - 12 months)

Birth – 6 months: Cries, coos in response to voice and smiles.
6 to 9 months: Babbles, responds to name, shouts to attract attention.
10-11 months: Imitates sounds, says "mama/papa" without meaning.
12 months: Says 1-2 words, points to objects.

Speech Milestones (13 months - 2 years)

13-15 months: Understands simple instructions, uses "mama", "papa" and other words.
16-18 months: Uses 10-20 words, starts combining words.
19-24 months: Uses 50 words, two-word phrases.

Speech Milestones (2-4 years)

2 – 2.5 years: Uses 400-450 words, two-three-word phrases.
2.5 – 3 years: Uses plurals, past tense, counts three objects.
3 – 4 years: Tells a story, knows colors, shapes, asks "why" and "who" questions.

Tips for Parents

  • Talk to your child from birth.
  • Respond to baby's coos and babbling.
  • Play simple games with your baby.
  • Use gestures along with words.
  • Expand on what your child says.
  • Look at family photos and talk about them.
  • Ask lots of simple questions.
  • Describe your child's daily activities.
